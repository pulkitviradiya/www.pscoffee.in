Section 01 · The observation

The observation that started this

The people who drink coffee every day in India are being asked to pay for the people who drink it once a week. The ₹300 latte works as a weekend treat — a destination, an occasion, something planned around. It does not work for a Tuesday morning before a 9am stand-up and a 3pm follow-up call.

The specialty cafe model — high rent, high labour, high ticket size — was built for an occasion. Every cost in that model assumes the customer is visiting once, maybe twice a week, and is willing to pay for the ambiance along with the cup. We built nothing for the people drinking coffee daily, because nothing in the model was designed for daily.

That gap is not a coincidence. It is a structural consequence of how Indian specialty coffee scaled — cafe first, daily habit second, if at all. We are building P.S. Coffee in the opposite order.

Section 02 · The format

Why the Pod format is the right answer

A Pod is 50–200 square feet inside a building that already has hundreds of people in it daily. No destination visit. No detour. No cover charge for the ambiance. The coffee earns its place because of what is in the cup, not what is around it.

The format is not a limitation — it is the pricing mechanism. Remove the rent premium of a standalone cafe, remove the table service, remove the ambiance investment, and the ₹300 latte's overhead collapses to something that can support a ₹89 specialty cup without touching the bean.

Section 03 · Why Gujarat, why now

Why Gujarat — and why now

Gujarat's coffee culture is where Mumbai's was six to seven years ago. The young urban professional class has formed. The gym culture is strong and growing. The co-working ecosystem is expanding fast, with new spaces opening across Ahmedabad, Surat, and Vadodara every quarter.

The competitive landscape, meanwhile, is empty. First Coffee is in Delhi-NCR. abCoffee is in Mumbai and Bengaluru. Nobody has planted a flag in Ahmedabad with a credible specialty grab-and-go brand. We are doing that — not because Gujarat is easier, but because it is the largest underserved market for daily-habit specialty coffee in the country.

What Ahmedabad already has in place

  • 11 IT parks — concentrated daily footfall of exactly the customer this format is built for.
  • 14+ major co-working spaces — IKube, Wing, Plug and Play, and others, all candidates for Pod hosting.
  • 4 significant university clusters — CEPT, Nirma, IIMA, MICA — a built-in pipeline of future daily drinkers.
  • One of India's most active gym cultures — a segment that already wants functional, no-nonsense coffee.

Frequently asked questions

Why did P.S. Coffee choose Gujarat over Mumbai, Delhi, or Bengaluru?+
Because those markets are already contested. abCoffee, First Coffee, and Drickle have all planted flags in at least one metro. Gujarat — with roughly 3.5 million urban professionals across Ahmedabad, Surat, and Vadodara — has zero grab-and-go specialty coffee chains operating at scale. It is the largest unclaimed market in the country.
Why Ahmedabad specifically, and not Surat or Vadodara first?+
Ahmedabad has the densest concentration of the target customer right now — 11 IT parks, 14+ co-working spaces, and 4 university clusters in one city. Surat and Vadodara are part of the same Gujarat thesis and are planned as the format proves out in Ahmedabad first.
Is P.S. Coffee operational yet?+
Not yet. We are pre-Pod. The website is live and in a traction-tracking phase, the brand and format are designed, and the first Pods are being planned. Updates will be shared via the newsletter as we get closer to opening.
How is this different from just copying abCoffee's model?+
The Pod format and the third-wave thesis are proven — we're not claiming otherwise. What's different is the geography and the local execution: sourcing 100% Indian Arabica, building for Gujarat's specific co-working and gym ecosystem, and starting where there's no incumbent to compete with for host buildings or customer attention.
What kind of co-founder or operator is P.S. Coffee looking for?+
Someone with QSR, retail, or supply-chain experience at scale in tier-1 Indian markets, who wants to build unit economics from the very first Pod rather than join an already-scaled operation. The Join Us page has more detail on open roles and partnership conversations.
How can I support this if I'm not an investor or operator?+
Join the newsletter or P.S. Pass waitlist to be among the first to know when Pods open near you, or get in touch via the Join Us page if you know a co-working space, gym, or campus that might want to host a Pod.
