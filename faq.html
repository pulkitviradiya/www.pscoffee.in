Plain & simple.

The same promise across every page: better daily coffee, closer to where you already are.

Brand.

P.S. Coffee is a specialty coffee brand built around compact Pods inside the places people already visit: offices, co-working spaces, campuses, gyms, and high-footfall buildings. The promise is 100% Arabica coffee from ₹89, made fresh and ready before you arrive.
P.S. stands for Plain and Simple. It also works like a postscript: the small, deliberate addition that completes the day. The brand is built to be useful without becoming theatre.
Not yet. P.S. Coffee is currently pre-launch and concept-stage. The website is being used to test demand, collect feedback, build partnerships, evaluate locations, and prepare the first Pods.
The brand is being built for Gujarat first, with Ahmedabad and nearby daily-work locations as the early focus. The site may reference broader India coffee trends, but the first operating thesis is local.

Pods.

A Pod is a compact specialty coffee station, usually designed for a 50-200 sq ft footprint. It is not a cafe and not a vending machine. It is a self-contained coffee operation with fresh preparation, trained staff, and a small footprint inside a host location.
The cafe is not the product; the cup is. Pods reduce rent, seating, and service overhead so the brand can keep the bean standard high while making specialty coffee viable as a daily habit.
Pods are designed for offices, corporate campuses, co-working spaces, universities, gyms, residential communities, and other daily-loop buildings with meaningful footfall.
Yes, if the building has strong daily footfall and a useful fit for coffee, matcha, or light food. Start with the Host a Pod page or write to hello@pscoffee.in.

Coffee & menu.

The coffee standard is 100% Arabica. The site references Indian Arabica, including Chikmagalur and Coorg, as the sourcing direction. No Robusta, no chicory, no filler is the stated standard.
Arabica is generally smoother, less bitter, and lower in caffeine than Robusta. For P.S., it is a quality standard: the cost savings should come from the Pod format, not by lowering the bean standard.
The thesis is lower overhead. A Pod does not carry the same rent, seating, ambience, and table-service cost structure as a full cafe. That is what makes a lower entry price possible without changing the bean promise.
Yes. The menu direction includes specialty coffee, matcha, cold drinks, and light food. Coffee is the anchor; the Pod is the format.
P.S. Matcha is the matcha line, built around properly prepared matcha drinks such as hot matcha latte, iced matcha latte, matcha tonic, and cold foam-style drinks. The page positions it as real matcha, not a sugary green shortcut.

Pack & app.

P.S. Pack is the subscription and prepaid coffee product. The idea is simple: pay once, drink daily, and let your Pack balance apply automatically at the Pod.
No. The site is concept-stage, so prices, Pack structures, validity, inclusions, taxes, and redemption rules may change before launch. Current pages show the intended direction, not a binding sale.
The app is designed around a three-tap order: open the app, pick a drink, confirm, and choose a pick-up time. The Pod prepares your cup fresh to that time so you do not stand in a normal queue.
Not yet. The app page currently collects interest so people can be notified when the app is live.
