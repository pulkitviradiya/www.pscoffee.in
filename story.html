We did not start with coffee. We started with a question.

Why does specialty coffee cost ₹300 in India? The beans are Indian. The baristas are Indian. The overhead is not.

Read the answer
The founding observation.

The cup was not broken. The format around it was.

Specialty coffee in India had become something you visited on a weekend, photographed once, and paid for like an occasion. But the people who needed better coffee most were not occasional drinkers. They were the daily ones.

A ₹300 latte does not cost ₹300 because the bean demands it. It costs that because the cafe has to recover rent, seating, service, slow queues, unused Wi-Fi, and the theatre of being a cafe. The daily drinker ends up paying for the room, not just the cup.

That was the question that started P.S. Coffee: what happens if we keep the bean standard and remove everything that does not improve the drink?

The answer became simple: specialty-grade beans, minimal overhead, honest technology, and Pods inside buildings people already visit. A cup that starts at ₹89 because the building is no longer hiding inside the price.

P.S. Psychology.

Why P.S.?

A postscript is the line you add after the main message is done. It is small, but often it is the line that tells the truth.

That is how coffee works in a day. It is not the meeting, the commute, the workout, or the deadline. It is the line added after all of that. The small thing that quietly makes the rest possible.

P.S. Coffee is built for that place in your day: not a performance, not a cafe visit, not a grand ritual. A better cup, exactly when you need it, without asking you to arrange your life around it.

Plain & simple.

Founder note.

This began as an irritation, not a business plan.

The irritation was simple: India grows serious coffee, hires serious baristas, and has serious daily drinkers. Yet the price kept telling people that specialty was an occasional luxury.

P.S. Coffee is the attempt to make that contradiction disappear. Not by making coffee cheaper in spirit, but by making the format more honest.
