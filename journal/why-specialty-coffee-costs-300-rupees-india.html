Why Specialty Coffee in India Still Costs ₹300 — And Where That Overhead Actually Goes

By P.S. Coffee June 2026 7 min read

A ₹300 latte in Ahmedabad contains about ₹18 worth of coffee. The other ₹282 funds the real estate, the table you did not sit at, the barista who also sweeps the floor, and the playlist in the background while you are trying to work.

This is not a criticism of specialty cafes. It is a structural observation. And it is exactly why, for millions of Indians who drink coffee every single day, specialty-grade coffee remains a weekend treat, not a Tuesday morning.

P.S. Coffee was started because we believed the ingredient standard and the pricing model were two entirely different problems. We solved the second one without touching the first.

The actual cost breakdown of a ₹300 latte.

Here is what a ₹300 latte costs to make in a premium specialty cafe in an Indian metro:

Cost componentAmount
Premium location rent, amortized₹40-60
Barista labour and benefits₹25-35
Specialty coffee beans, 100% Arabica₹18-25
Packaging: cup, sleeve, lid, napkin₹8-12
Equipment power and maintenance₹10-15
Store overhead: water, utilities, Wi-Fi₹8-12
Gross profit margin₹30-50
GST at 5%₹18-22

Total actual cost: ₹149-204. Retail price: ₹300. The cup is not the expensive part. The room around the cup is.

The cup: honest. The overhead: removed.

Why the Indian coffee market is shifting now.

India's ready-to-drink coffee market is projected to reach $20 billion by 2030. The shift happened quietly: urban professionals under 35 now drink coffee daily because work changed. Co-working culture. Longer work cycles. Early gym routines. Instagram made specialty visible. The daily habit made it urgent.

This cohort has a daily coffee habit. But the existing pricing model cannot serve them affordably. A ₹300 latte is aspirational, not habitual. The math breaks at scale. That is when markets shift.

The white space nobody has filled in Gujarat.

Three specialty cafe chains dominate national awareness: Blue Tokai, Third Wave, and abCoffee. None has a significant presence in Gujarat.

Ahmedabad, Surat, and Vadodara together represent a large urban professional base. Zero grab-and-go specialty coffee chains. Not one.

That is the white space P.S. Coffee is built for. The model is proven. The customer need exists. The supply is missing.
